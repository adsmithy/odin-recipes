Meatball-Inspired Meatloaf

Chef John's Meatball-Inspired Meatloaf: An Oldie But A Goodie

This is chef John's famous meatloaf, inspired by his equally-famous meatball marinara dish. During the Great Depression, this type of dish was a popular method for creating a filling meal for your entire family using what little meat you had available.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 onion diced onion
  • salt to taste
  • minced cloves of garlic
  • 1 ½ cups very dry white bread crumbs
  • 1 ½ cups milk
  • 2 pounds ground chuck
  • ½ cup chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 2 ounces grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 3 cups tomato sauce

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees
  2. Heat olive oil and butter together in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Saute onion with a pinch of salt in hot oil and butter until onion is golden brown and soft, 5 to 10 minutes. Add garlic and saute until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Place bread crumbs in a large bowl. Add milk and stir with a fork until combined. Let sit until all of the milk is absorbed into the breadcrumbs, 15 to 20 minutes.
  4. Combine ground chuck, cooled onion mixture, parsley, Parmesan cheese, eggs, 2 teaspoons salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper in a bowl.
  5. Shape meat into a 3x4x10-inch meatloaf. Pour tomato sauce over the loaf and into the bottom of the dish.
  6. Bake until no longer pink in the center, about 1 hour 10 minutes. Center should read at least 160 degrees. Let meatloaf rest for at least 10 minutes before slicing.
